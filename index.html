Why

My father taught me integrity. My mother taught me service. Schools grounded me in fundamentals, while also seeding the instinct to question. College too was an environment of fierce competitive plausibility, and law school ultimately revealed how stories shape outcomes, how power moves through systems, and the inextricability of well-being and infrastructure.

It's been a lifelong dedication to deep understanding, or the attempt thereof, which has allowed me to see patterns across cultures, disciplines, and industries. What I keep coming back to are the building blocks: preserved reasoning, ecosystem balance, and strong communities of people.

Each generation has their issue. Ours—non-ironically—is about our ability to maintain individual coherence under pressure, and for organizations to put prosperity in alignment with human values. These are the things we need to address, because without them we're not attuned for our greatest potential.

I apply this thinking to my areas of exposure. Please feel free to connect.
